The Complete Sea Urchin (Uni) Buyer's Guide 2026

Sea urchin — known as uni in Japanese — has become one of the most sought-after delicacies in Hong Kong and Macau's fine dining scene. Whether you run an omakase counter, a Japanese restaurant, or a 5-star hotel, understanding how to buy, grade, and store uni is essential to delivering a consistently excellent guest experience.

What Makes Sea Urchin Special

Uni is the edible gonads (roe) of the sea urchin. When fresh and properly graded, it has a rich, creamy texture with a complex flavor that balances sweetness, brininess, and the subtle taste of the ocean. The flavor profile varies significantly between species, origin, and season — which is why sourcing knowledge matters so much. Poor-quality or old uni is bitter and mushy, and can damage your restaurant's reputation. Top-quality uni elevates any dish it touches.

Sea Urchin Varieties: Which Is Best for Your Menu?

Bafun Uni (馬糞海膽) from Hokkaido, Japan is the gold standard for omakase. Small, golden lobes with an intensely sweet, rich flavor — available in AA and A grade. Peak season is June through August.

Murasaki Uni (紫海膽) from Japan offers a lighter, more delicate flavor with clean ocean finish. Available year-round, making it the reliable workhorse for daily service.

Canadian Red Sea Urchin (加拿大紅海膽) from British Columbia features large, bold lobes with a buttery flavor profile. Peak season October through May aligns perfectly with the Hokkaido off-season, enabling year-round premium supply.

Maine Atlantic Uni from the US East Coast provides a seasonal alternative with its own distinct flavor character.

Understanding Sea Urchin Grading: AA, A, and B

AA Grade is omakase-only: the finest lobes, vivid golden-orange color, firm yet creamy texture, zero bitterness, minimal liquid. Only a fraction of any harvest qualifies. Priced accordingly.

A Grade suits restaurant excellence — consistent quality, beautiful presentation, appropriate for premium nigiri, sashimi platters, and featured dishes.

B Grade is for culinary applications: sauces, pasta, risotto, dressings. The flavor is good but the appearance or texture may not meet raw presentation standards.

When evaluating uni, examine color (bright golden-orange), firmness (holds shape, not mushy), aroma (fresh ocean, no ammonia), and liquid content (minimal is best).

Seasonality and Supply Planning

Hokkaido Bafun Uni peaks June–August: this is the most celebrated uni season, when the finest AA-grade product is available in limited quantities. Restaurants should plan menus and reserve allocations in advance.

Year-round supply strategy: source Murasaki Uni from Japan for consistent daily service, supplement with Canadian Red (Oct–May) for premium variety, and reserve Hokkaido Bafun for seasonal features and omakase.

Professional Storage for Maximum Shelf Life

Sea urchin is highly perishable. Proper storage is non-negotiable:

• Temperature: 2°C–4°C (never frozen unless purchasing processed product) • Orientation: store trays flat, never stacked or tilted • Avoid pressure: do not place anything on top of uni trays • Use within 2–3 days of delivery for raw service • Keep packaging sealed until service • Drain excess liquid carefully before plating

Rejection protocol: if uni arrives with ammonia smell, excessive liquid, or collapsed lobes — reject the delivery. We offer 48-hour replacement guarantee for any quality issues.
