3:T63f,Hong Kong operates under the Food Safety Ordinance (FSO), enforced by the Food and Environmental Hygiene Department (FEHD). Key requirements for seafood:

**Import & Registration Requirements**
- All seafood must be imported through licensed importers
- Importers must submit certificates of origin and health certificates
- Products are subject to random testing at the border
- High-risk items (raw seafood for sashimi/oysters) get additional scrutiny
- Any batch failing testing is quarantined and destroyed

**Supplier Documentation**
You must maintain:
- Importer's license and certificate
- Certificate of origin (from farm/harvest location)
- Health certificate (issued by exporting country's authority)
- Test results proving freedom from bacterial/viral contamination
- Traceability documentation (farm identification, harvest date)

Missing documentation = FEHD can refuse entry or conduct store inspections

**Temperature Control**
- Seafood must maintain 0-4°C (32-39°F) during transport
- Storage at point of sale: 0-4°C maximum
- Violations result in warnings, fines, or license suspension

**Handling & Preparation**
- Raw seafood (sashimi, oysters) must come from approved suppliers on approved lists
- No cross-contamination with other foods
- Separate cutting boards, utensils for raw seafood
- Staff training on food safety mandatory

**Record-Keeping**
HKFEHD requires:
- Supplier records (with contact information)
- Batch/lot numbers
- Receipt dates and quantities
- Temperature logs
- Staff training records
- Cleaning and sanitation logs

12:T41c,If you operate in both jurisdictions (many hotel chains, restaurant groups do), you need dual compliance:

**Different Approval Processes**
Approving a supplier in HK doesn't mean it's approved in Macau (and vice versa). You must:

1. Get supplier approved in HK by FEHD
2. Separately apply for approval in Macau
3. Maintain separate documentation
4. Use different import channels (can't cross-import between HK and Macau without reapproval)

**Practical Strategy**
Most restaurant groups solve this by:
- Using HK-approved suppliers for HK operations
- Using Macau-approved suppliers for Macau operations
- Using international suppliers (e.g., Japan, Norway) who have pre-approval in both jurisdictions

International suppliers with both approvals cost 10-20% premium but eliminate cross-border hassle.

**Physical Movement of Product**
You cannot:
- Move product from HK operation to Macau without customs clearance
- Pool inventory between HK and Macau
- Combine shipments from HK for use in Macau

14:T6d7,Based on FEHD and Macau authority inspection data:

**Issue 1: Missing Supplier Documentation**
Most common violation. Result: Warnings, fines, product seizure

How to avoid:
- Require all suppliers to provide documentation BEFORE first delivery
- Create a checklist: origin certificate, health certificate, test results, importer license
- Keep copies organized by supplier
- Update annually (some documents expire)

**Issue 2: Temperature Control Failures**
Second most common. Result: Product recall, license suspension risk

How to avoid:
- Invest in reliable refrigeration (no cheap units)
- Install thermometers in all seafood storage areas
- Log temperatures every 2 hours during operating hours
- Train all staff on temperature requirements
- Have backup plan for refrigeration failure

**Issue 3: Cross-Contamination**
Third most common. Result: Major fines, possible closure

How to avoid:
- Separate cutting boards (color-code: red for raw, white for cooked)
- Separate utensils
- Dedicated prep area for raw seafood
- Clean between uses (not just wipe)
- Train staff on why this matters (not just rules)

**Issue 4: Inadequate Staff Training**
Often cited in inspection reports. Result: Warnings, mandatory retraining

- Complete formal food safety course (not just on-the-job training)
- Certificate on file

16:T57d,Raw seafood has extra regulations because of bacterial/viral risk:

**Approved Supplier List (ASL)**
Both HK and Macau maintain official ASL for raw seafood
- Only products from ASL suppliers can be served raw
- Using non-ASL supplier for raw seafood = immediate violation
- ASL is published online; check it before adding to menu

**Freezing Requirements**
Some raw seafood requires freezing to kill parasites:
- Sashimi-grade fish: must be frozen to -4°C or lower for 7 days
- Oysters: varies by origin; check supplier's certification
- Uni: varies; typically not frozen requirement if sourced correctly

Failure to freeze = liability for parasitic infection

**Storage Separation**
Raw seafood must be stored separately from:
- Cooked seafood
- Meat
- Vegetables
- All other ingredients

Reason: Any cross-contamination risk eliminates the 'raw ready' status

**Staff Certification**
Staff handling raw seafood should have additional training on:
- Temperature control
- Contamination prevention
- Parasitic risk
- What to do if product looks unusual

Invest in specialized training (cost: HK$500-1,500 per person)

**Communication to Customers**
Many jurisdictions require disclosure when serving raw seafood:
- 'This product contains raw fish'
- 'This product may contain parasites'
- Recommended in HK; required in some Macau establishments
