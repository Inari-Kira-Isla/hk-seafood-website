3:Td56,Most restaurants calculate seafood costs as 'wholesale price per kg.' This is incomplete. True seafood COGS includes multiple layers.

**The Full Cost Equation:**

```
Seafood COGS = Wholesale Price + Freight + Handling + Wastage + Cold-Chain + Labor
```

**1. Wholesale Price (60-70% of total cost)**
Example: Canadian Red Sea Urchin
- Base price: HKD 800/kg
- This is your starting point

**2. Freight & Logistics (8-12% of total cost)**
Often hidden in wholesale pricing, but real:
- Air freight surcharges: HKD 50-100/kg for priority delivery
- Cold-chain logistics: HKD 30-50/kg for specialized handling
- Customs clearance: HKD 20-30/kg for import fees
- Example: HKD 100-180/kg in additional logistics costs

**3. Handling & Waste (10-15% of total cost)**
Losses from receiving to plate:
- Quality inspection rejects: 2-3% of incoming volume (grading variation)
- Preparation waste (shells, membrane, inedible portions): 15-20% typical
- Spoilage from temperature fluctuations: 1-2% if not careful
- Trimming and portioning: 5-10% loss typical

Example calculation:
- Order 10kg Canadian Red Sea Urchin
- Receive 10kg; inspect and grade
- Reject 200g (2%) due to quality — loss HKD 160
- Use 8kg (20% is membrane/waste) — effective yield is 8kg
- Trim and portion for service — 400g additional loss
- Effective yield: 7.6kg from 10kg order (24% total loss)

Waste cost: HKD 800 × 10kg = HKD 8,000 cost for 7.6kg product = effective HKD 1,053/kg

**4. Cold-Chain Management (3-5% of total cost)**
Energy and equipment to maintain quality:
- Dedicated refrigerator maintenance and temperature monitoring
- Ice and cold packs for delivery
- Daily rotation and FIFO tracking labor
- Backup cooling if primary fails

Example: HKD 1,000/month cold-chain costs ÷ 100kg monthly seafood = HKD 10/kg overhead

**5. Labor: Receiving, Inspection, Prep (5-10% of total cost)**
People costs to handle incoming inventory:
- Receiving and inspecting deliveries: 0.25 hours per delivery = HKD 150
- Grading and quality assessment: 0.5 hours per order = HKD 300
- Prep (cleaning, portioning, plating): varies by dish (see labor cost below)
- FIFO rotation and inventory tracking: 0.5 hours weekly = HKD 75

Example: HKD 525 labor/delivery on 10kg order = HKD 52.50/kg

**True COGS Example: Canadian Red Sea Urchin**
```
Wholesale price:           HKD 800/kg (base)
Freight/logistics:       + HKD 150/kg (air freight, customs, handling)
Waste (24% loss):        + HKD 253/kg (800 × 24% ÷ 76% yield)
Cold-chain overhead:     + HKD 10/kg
Labor (handling/prep):   + HKD 53/kg
────────────────────────────
True COGS:               HKD 1,266/kg
────────────────────────────
```

This means:
- Your true cost is 58% higher than the wholesale price
- A HKD 800/kg quote is actually HKD 1,266/kg when fully costed
- Pricing HKD 1,200/kg thinking you're making margin? You're losing money

**Cost Variation by Product:**

Higher-waste products:
- Sea urchin (uni): 20-25% waste (membrane removal)
- Live lobster: 35-40% waste (head, shells)
- Whole fish: 30-35% waste (head, bones, guts)

Lower-waste products:
- Salmon fillet: 3-5% waste (portioning trim)
- Scallop meat: 5-8% waste (side muscle)
- Pre-portioned products: 1-2% waste

c:T7cc,Restaurant operators confuse gross margin and net margin. This costs money.

**Gross Margin = Revenue - COGS (raw material cost)**

Example: Sea Urchin Sashimi
- Plate sells for: HKD 380
- COGS (true cost, calculated above): HKD 127 (based on 10% portion of HKD 1,266/kg)
- Gross margin: HKD 380 - HKD 127 = HKD 253
- Gross margin %: HKD 253 ÷ HKD 380 = 66.6%

Sounds excellent. But this is before:
- Soy sauce, wasabi, garnish
- Plate, bowl, ice
- Chef labor to plate
- Rent, utilities, insurance
- Marketing, delivery, etc.

**Net Margin = (Revenue - COGS - Operating Costs) ÷ Revenue**

For typical Hong Kong restaurants:
- Gross Margin Target: 65-70% (after all food costs)
- Operating Costs: 35-40% (rent, labor, utilities)
- Net Margin (Profit): 25-35%

This means:
- A HKD 380 dish with HKD 127 COGS leaves HKD 253 gross
- Operating costs take ~HKD 140 of that (35%)
- Net profit: ~HKD 113 per plate (30%)

**The Real Question: Does Your Seafood Pricing Support This?**

Many restaurants under-price premium seafood because they don't understand true COGS.

Example: Premium sea urchin sashimi
- Wholesale quoted: HKD 800/kg
- Chef says: "That's cheap, let's price at HKD 380/portion"
- True COGS: HKD 1,266/kg (66% higher!)
- At 10g portion: HKD 127 per plate
- Gross margin needed for 65% target: HKD 127 ÷ 0.35 = HKD 363 revenue
- Actual price: HKD 380 (barely hitting margin target)

Once operating costs apply, profit is thin. One spike in waste or labor cost, and you lose money on the dish.

**Solution: Cost + Margin = Price**

Don't accept wholesale quotes at face value. Calculate true COGS first.

```
True COGS per portion: HKD 127
Desired gross margin: 65% (means 35% max COGS ratio)
Required revenue: HKD 127 ÷ 0.35 = HKD 363
Target price (with 5-10% luxury markup): HKD 420-450
Actually competitive in Hong Kong premium market: YES
```

This is why Michelin-starred restaurants charge HKD 450+ for sea urchin sashimi. d:Taef,Two pricing philosophies: cost-based (what you pay + markup) vs. value-based (what diners will pay).

**Cost-Based Pricing (Most Common, Often Wrong)**

Formula: COGS × Markup Multiple = Selling Price

Example:
- COGS: HKD 127
- Standard restaurant markup: 3x
- Price: HKD 381

Problem: Ignores market value. If diners will pay HKD 500 for that dish, you're leaving money on the table.

**Value-Based Pricing (Strategic Restaurants, Higher Margin)**

Approach: Research what market will pay, then back-calculate margin

```
Dish: Uni (Sea Urchin) Sashimi (3-piece, 12g total)

Market Research (February 2026, Hong Kong):
- 3-star Michelin: HKD 520-680 per portion
- High-end omakase: HKD 420-520 per portion
- Fine dining: HKD 320-420 per portion
- Casual premium: HKD 220-320 per portion
- Target positioning: High-end omakase
- Optimal price: HKD 450

Margin Calculation:
- Revenue: HKD 450
- COGS: HKD 127
- Gross margin: HKD 323 (71.8%)
- This exceeds 65% target — profitable and competitive
```

**Value-Based Pricing Factors for Hong Kong/Macau:**

**1. Restaurant Positioning**
- Michelin-starred: HKD 450-680 per seafood course
- High-end omakase counter: HKD 350-500
- Fine dining (hotel/casino): HKD 280-400
- Casual premium (neighborhood): HKD 200-350

**2. Product Rarity/Seasonality**
- Year-round available (Atlantic salmon): Standard pricing
- Seasonal (Hokkaido uni, June-July): +15-25% premium
- Limited allocation (King salmon): +40-80% premium
- Harvest-dependent (spot prawns, March-April): +50-100% premium

**3. Preparation Complexity**
- Simple plating (sashimi on ice): Base price
- Moderate prep (sauce, garnish, warmth): +10-15%
- High prep (multiple components, technique): +25-40%
- Show/theater element (table plating): +30-50%

**4. Guest Perception of Value**
- Transparency ("Norwegian salmon, today's catch"): +10%
- Certification (MSC, ASC visible): +5-10%
- Story (fishery name, harvest date): +10-15%
- Rarity communication ("only available February"): +15-25%

**5. Competitive Benchmarking**
- Research 3-5 direct competitors
- Identify price ranges for similar dishes
- Position 10-15% premium if differentiation exists

**Example: Premium Salmon Sashimi Pricing**
```
Base COGS (Atlantic salmon, 20g): HKD 95
Competitor research:
- Restaurant A (Michelin): HKD 420
- Restaurant B (High-end omakase): HKD 380
- Restaurant C (Fine dining): HKD 320
- Average: HKD 373

Your positioning: Premium omakase counter, Hong Kong location
Margin target: 65%
Required price: HKD 95 ÷ 0.35 = HKD 271 minimum

Value-based approach:
- Competitive price: HKD 380 (matches high-end omakase)
- Margin check: (HKD 380 - HKD 95) ÷ HKD 380 = 75% gross margin ✓
- Profitability: Excellent at HKD 380

Final decision: HKD 380 (matches market, achieves margin, defensible)
