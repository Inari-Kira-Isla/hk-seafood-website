Salmon Varieties — Atlantic, King, Sockeye for Hong Kong & Macau

Choosing the right salmon variety depends on your menu concept, price point, and service style. Here is everything you need to know about the three premium salmon varieties Inari Global Food supplies to Hong Kong and Macau's restaurants and hotels.

大西洋三文魚

Atlantic Salmon — 挪威、蘇格蘭、法羅群島

Most Popular
Flavor

Mild, buttery, consistently rich. Approachable flavor that works universally — raw, grilled, or smoked.

Texture

Medium-firm, moist. Holds well for sashimi and easily portions for nigiri.

Color

Bright orange-pink with consistent fat marbling

Season

Year-round. Air-freighted 2–3 times per week.

Best Uses
SashimiNigiriTatakiGrillingHotel buffetsAll-purpose
Ideal For

The default premium salmon for any restaurant serving sashimi. Consistent quality, year-round availability, competitive pricing.

Premium
Flavor

Richest flavor of all salmon species. Deeply buttery, complex, intensely satisfying. The 'wagyu of salmon.'

Texture

Softer than Atlantic, extremely high fat content. Melts on the palate.

Color

Deep orange-red, intensely marbled with white fat lines

Season

Seasonal — inquire for current availability.

Best Uses
Premium omakaseHigh-end nigiriChef's tasting menusSeasonal specials
Ideal For

For omakase programs and fine dining menus that command premium pricing. Guests familiar with uni and premium seafood will recognize and appreciate the difference.

Seasonal
Flavor

Stronger, more assertive flavor than Atlantic. Clean ocean character with firm, robust taste.

Texture

Firm, lean. Lower fat than Atlantic or King. Holds well when cooked.

Deep red flesh — the most visually striking salmon

Salmon Variety FAQs

Which salmon is best for sashimi in Hong Kong?

Atlantic Salmon from Norway is the most widely used for sashimi in Hong Kong — it offers consistent quality, bright color, and mild flavor that works for all customer palates. For premium omakase applications, King Salmon (Chinook) offers unmatched richness. Both are available from Inari Global Food.

What is the difference between Norwegian salmon and Scottish salmon?

Both are Atlantic Salmon (Salmo salar) farmed in cold North Atlantic waters. Norwegian salmon is the highest volume and most price-competitive. Scottish salmon from the Highlands is prized for its slightly more complex flavor from the fast-flowing cold rivers used in farming. Both are sashimi-grade. Faroe Islands salmon is similarly premium.

Is King Salmon available year-round in Hong Kong?

King Salmon (Chinook) is seasonal. New Zealand King Salmon is available for a significant portion of the year; Alaskan wild King Salmon has a shorter window. Contact Inari Global Food to check current availability.
