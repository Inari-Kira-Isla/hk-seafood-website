What is the difference between Bafun Uni and Murasaki Uni?

Bafun Uni (馬糞海膽) from Hokkaido has rich, intensely sweet flavor with golden color — considered the premium variety. Murasaki Uni (紫海膽) is lighter and more delicate with a clean ocean taste. Bafun is rarer and more expensive.

How is sea urchin quality graded?

Sea urchin is graded AA, A, and B. AA Grade has perfect golden color, firm texture, zero blemishes — reserved for top omakase. A Grade is excellent for sushi restaurants. B Grade suits cooking applications like pasta and sauces.

How should I store fresh sea urchin?

Keep fresh uni at 0–2°C at all times. Consume within 2–3 days of delivery. Never freeze fresh uni as texture degrades significantly. Store in the original wooden tray, covered, away from ice or water contact.

What is the best season for Japanese sea urchin?

Hokkaido Bafun Uni peaks June–August. Murasaki Uni is available year-round with varying peaks by region. Canadian Red Sea Urchin peaks October–May, providing excellent quality during Japanese off-season months.

Canadian Red Sea Urchin from British Columbia produces large, bold-flavored lobes with a buttery texture. While different from Hokkaido Bafun Uni, top Canadian grades are used in Hong Kong and Macau's finest restaurants as an excellent alternative.

What is the difference between Atlantic salmon and King salmon?

Atlantic Salmon (大西洋三文魚) from Norway and Scotland is mild, buttery, and consistently marbled — the most popular sashimi choice in Hong Kong. King Salmon (帝王三文魚 / Chinook) has the highest oil content of all species, with an intensely rich, buttery flavor — preferred for premium omakase.
